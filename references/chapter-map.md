# Chapter Map

Use this map to connect a user's situation to the thirteen chapters. Treat chapter names as lenses, not rigid categories.

| 篇章 | Giles 1910 chapter name | 和平应用 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 计篇 | Laying Plans | Start with first principles: shared purpose, timing, terrain, leadership, rules, and realistic comparison before action. |
| 作战篇 | Waging War | Count the cost of prolonged conflict; prefer fast de-escalation, resource preservation, and avoiding exhaustion. |
| 谋攻篇 | Attack by Stratagem | Whole victory: solve the issue without destroying trust, people, reputation, or future cooperation. |
| 形篇 | Tactical Dispositions | Build an unwinnable-to-lose position first: clarity, documentation, support, options, and calm. |
| 势篇 | Energy | Design momentum through structure, incentives, timing, and team alignment instead of heroic individual effort. |
| 虚实篇 | Weak Points and Strong | Do not collide with hardened positions; find unmet needs, ambiguity, timing gaps, or low-resistance paths. |
| 军争篇 | Maneuvering | Manage movement, tradeoffs, coordination, and communication so progress does not create new disorder. |
| 九变篇 | Variation in Tactics | Adapt to conditions; avoid rule-bound action when context has changed. |
| 行军篇 | The Army on the March | Read signals, morale, environment, and risk before interpreting behavior or making commitments. |
| 地形篇 | Terrain | Match action to terrain: authority, incentives, constraints, culture, process, and relationship history. |
| 九地篇 | The Nine Situations | Identify the pressure state people are in; choose different moves for low-stakes, trapped, open, or desperate contexts. |
| 火攻篇 | The Attack by Fire | Avoid anger-driven action; use forceful escalation only when necessary, bounded, lawful, and proportionate. |
| 用间篇 | The Use of Spies | Seek reliable information ethically; understand interests through listening, observation, and trusted channels. |

## Short classical anchors

- `兵者，国之大事`: Conflict is consequential; investigate before acting.
- `经之以五事`: Diagnose through purpose, timing, terrain, leadership, and method.
- `不战而屈人之兵`: The best resolution removes resistance without destructive confrontation.
- `知彼知己，百战不殆`: Understand both sides to avoid needless danger.
- `先为不可胜，以待敌之可胜`: Make yourself stable first; wait for a real opening.
- `胜兵先胜而后求战`: Create success conditions before the decisive conversation.
- `兵无常势，水无常形`: Strategy must adapt to the shape of the situation.
- `主不可以怒而兴师`: Do not act from anger; preserve what cannot be restored.

## Translation handling

When using English, prefer brief public-domain Giles wording or paraphrase. If comparing translations, label uncertainty and focus on strategic meaning rather than proving a single perfect rendering.
