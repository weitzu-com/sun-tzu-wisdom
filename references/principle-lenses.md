# Principle Lenses

Use these lenses to transform ancient strategic ideas into peaceful diagnosis and action.

## Whole victory

Anchor: `全国为上` and `不战而屈人之兵`.

Modern meaning: The best outcome preserves what matters: trust, optionality, reputation, energy, and long-term cooperation. A "win" that leaves resentment, burnout, or reputational damage is incomplete.

Apply by asking:
- What must remain intact after this problem is solved?
- What outcome would make punishment, exposure, or confrontation unnecessary?
- Can the system be redesigned so the conflict no longer repeats?

## Five factors

Anchor: `道、天、地、将、法`.

Modern meaning:
- `道`: shared purpose, legitimacy, values, stakeholder alignment.
- `天`: timing, urgency, cycles, mood, external conditions.
- `地`: constraints, distance, authority, access, environment.
- `将`: judgment, credibility, courage, empathy, discipline.
- `法`: rules, process, roles, incentives, resources.

Apply by diagnosing which factor is weakest. Most workplace and relationship problems are not solved by more pressure; they are solved by restoring one missing factor.

## Know both sides

Anchor: `知彼知己`.

Modern meaning: Do not reduce the other party to a label. Understand their incentives, fears, constraints, information, and face-saving needs while also knowing your own limits and blind spots.

Apply by producing two maps:
- Their map: goals, fears, pressures, likely interpretation of your behavior.
- Your map: real need, non-negotiables, leverage, emotional triggers, fallback.

## First become hard to defeat

Anchor: `先为不可胜`.

Modern meaning: Before a difficult conversation or decision, stabilize your own position. Clarify facts, document commitments, gather support, lower emotional reactivity, and identify fallback options.

Apply by recommending preparation before confrontation:
- Write the facts in neutral language.
- Decide the minimum acceptable outcome.
- Prepare one collaborative ask and one boundary.
- Choose a calm time and channel.

## Avoid strength, address emptiness

Anchor: `避实击虚`.

Modern meaning: Do not attack where people are most defended. Address the gap: unclear ownership, missing information, unmet need, bad process, wrong timing, or unspoken fear.

Apply by replacing direct blame with low-resistance entry points:
- "Can we align on the goal?"
- "What constraint is making this hard?"
- "What would let us move one step forward?"

## Momentum over force

Anchor: `势`.

Modern meaning: Good strategy uses structure so the right behavior becomes easier. Change the meeting format, incentives, sequence, deadline, visibility, or decision rule instead of relying on willpower.

Apply by designing momentum:
- Make the next step small.
- Put the decision in writing.
- Create shared review points.
- Move from debate to experiment.

## Adaptation

Anchor: `因利制权` and `兵无常势`.

Modern meaning: Strategy is not a script. Keep the purpose steady and adapt the method as information changes.

Apply by offering branches:
- If they engage, collaborate.
- If they avoid, narrow the ask.
- If they escalate, pause and move to process.
- If trust is unsafe, use formal boundaries.

## Do not move from anger

Anchor: `主不可以怒而兴师，将不可以愠而致战`.

Modern meaning: Anger can signal a boundary, but it should not command the action. Delay irreversible moves until the aim, cost, and proportionality are clear.

Apply by recommending:
- Wait before sending heated messages.
- Draft privately, then rewrite for facts and requests.
- Separate emotional validation from strategic action.
- Preserve evidence and seek help if safety or legality is involved.
